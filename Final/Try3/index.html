ELEAGUE returns to Mortal Kombat 11 with new weekly 'Showdown' series beginning this Wednesday

ELEAGUE is continuing their Mortal Kombat 11 Showdown event with a new weekly series, the organization announced this morning.

This live streaming competition will see prominent members of the community diving into NetherRealm Studios' newly released fighting title with the goal of scoring as many wins as possible within a four-hour period each time. Competitors will aim to climb the leaderboards, and there will be cash prizes on the line.

The ELEAGUE Mortal Kombat 11 Showdown will come in the form of a 13-week competition that will begin on Wednesday, May 1, at 5 p.m. EDT / 2 p.m. PDT on Twitch and ELEAGUE.com. We'll be seeing live episodes up through Wednesday, July 24, and David "UltraDavid" Graham and Ryan "Mr. Aquaman" Kablik will return to commentate various matches across the players' live streams.

If you want to keep track of several competitors at one time, ELEAGUE has you covered. The organization has also announced the launch of their "Multi-Viewer Experience" feature that allows fans to "seamlessly switch between any of the influencers’ Twitch channels at any time during the weekly live Showdown competitions."

This interface will be available on ELEAGUE.com, and if you opt to use it you will be able to jump from the ELEAGUETV channel to any Showdown competitor stream with virtually no latency, according to the press release, and bring your multi-stream viewing experience all to one screen.

The ELEAGUE Mortal Kombat 11 Showdown sees competitors battle for wins against online opponents to earn points. In the initial, one-day Shodown event, each win earned players one point. They also scored five bonus points for winning a match with each of the Mortal Kombat 11 beta's five characters — as the event took place during the game's pre-order beta test period.


Previously, the players who were brought in to join the Showdown were BC|HoneyBee, Big D, Llumiya, Unrooolie, tru3ta1ent, Dragon, Killer Xinok, END|Scar, Mym Alkapone, Reo, PND|Ketchup and PND|Mustard. Though we have yet to see the list of competitors for the new weekly series, there's a chance that some of these players may return and other prominent figures, like FOX|SonicFox, might also be asked to join.

Come to Papa 3

This weekend is seeing a large Super Smash Bros. event taking place in Orlando, Florida.

Some of the big players set to attend include Liquid|Hungrybox, Armada|Myran, PG|Zain, eU|Samsora, RCS|Fatality, Wizzrobe, CND|Raito, WBG|MVD, SS|Colbol, 20GX|Gahtzu, 8BitMan, Typo|Sonido, MVG|ScAtt and Spiral|Ryo, though there are of course many more showing up as well.

There are so many, in fact, that Super Smash Bros. Ultimate alone has almost 500 entrants, while Super Smash Bros. Melee is the second biggest with 100 entrants joining up.

This event will have both Singles and Doubles events for the aforementioned Smash games, as well as Super Smash Bros. 64, Project M and Super Smash Bros. Brawl.

It's not all Smash, though, since the event also features Mortal Kombat 11, Dragon Ball FighterZ, Street Fighter 5: Arcade Edition, Tekken 7, BlazBlue: Cross Tag Battle, Soul Calibur 6 and Under Night In-Birth EXE: Late[st].

Streaming is being done at VGBootCamp, Polarity GG, Duo Studios and SSB64.
