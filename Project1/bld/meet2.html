"In 1670, the growing town of tamford was in need of a new Meeting House. The town originally voted that the new building be made of stone.

The building was 30 feet square and atop the 20' high exterior walls was built a roof of 2 diminishing stories, like a funnel or pyramid, and was crowned by a cupola. During its years of service, the Seccond Meeting House was also utilized as the town's fortification."
